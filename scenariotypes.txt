The following is a summary of the **44 interactive scenarios** used in the Bench2Drive benchmark, including their names and definitions as described in the source material.

# Bench2Drive Benchmark Scenarios

| # | Scenario Name | Definition / Description |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |

## Unused in this corpus

The following 10 of the original Bench2Drive 44 types have **zero matches**
across all 250 labeled scenarios (`scenario_labels.md`), confirmed across
two full corpus expansions (150→200→250 scenarios, +2 new directories)
with no new matches appearing either time. They remain the canonical
Bench2Drive definitions — kept here, not deleted, so the file stays usable
outside this project and so a future corpus expansion (e.g. a directory
drawn from closed-course control-loss tests) can still use them. See
`plan.md` Part 1 for the full rationale.

| # | Scenario Name | Definition / Description | Why unused in this corpus |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| 1 | **ControlLoss** | The ego vehicle loses control due to poor road conditions and must recover to its original lane. | Requires icy/poor-traction road conditions causing loss of control; absent from all 250 descriptions. |
| 3 | **ParkingCutIn** | The ego vehicle must slow down or brake for a vehicle exiting a parallel parking bay that cuts in front. | Requires a cut-in specifically caused by a vehicle exiting a parking bay; this corpus's parking-exit and cut-in scenarios never combine that way (they route to ParkingExit or StaticCutIn separately). |
| 13 | **VehiclesDooropenTwoWays** | The ego vehicle must maneuver to avoid a parked vehicle that has opened its door into the lane. | Requires a parked vehicle's door opening into the lane; no source scenario describes this. |
| 17 | **YieldToEmergencyVehicle** | The ego vehicle must maneuver to allow an emergency vehicle coming from behind to pass. | Requires an emergency vehicle (police/ambulance) approaching from behind; absent from all 250 descriptions. |
| 29 | **EnterActorFlows** | Special vehicles (e.g., police, ambulances) run a red light, forcing the ego vehicle to react or merge. | Requires a special/emergency vehicle running a red light; absent from all 250 descriptions. |
| 33 | **InterurbanActorFlow** | The ego vehicle turns left off an interurban road, crossing a fast traffic flow. | Requires an interurban road context; absent from all 250 descriptions. |
| 34 | **InterurbanAdvancedActorFlow** | The ego vehicle turns left onto an interurban road, crossing one fast flow and merging into another. | Requires an interurban road context; absent from all 250 descriptions. |
| 36 | **CrossingBicycleFlow** | The ego vehicle must turn at an intersection while yielding to bicycles crossing from the left. | Requires bicycles crossing at a junction the ego must yield to while turning; absent from all 250 descriptions (bicycle scenarios in this corpus map to DynamicObjectCrossing instead). |
| 41 | **VinillaSignalizedTurnEncounterGreenLight** | A basic scenario for passing through a signalized junction on a green light. | Requires a basic signalized-junction-on-green-light scenario with no stated conflict; absent from all 250 descriptions. |
| 42 | **VinillaSignalizedTurnEncounterRedLight** | A basic scenario for passing through a signalized junction when the light turns red to green. | Requires a basic signalized-junction-red-to-green scenario with no stated conflict; absent from all 250 descriptions. |

# Supplementary Scenario Types (proposed, beyond the original Bench2Drive 44)

The 44 types above are the original Bench2Drive benchmark set. While
labeling `text-only/`, `text-image/`, `text-video/`, and `image-only/`
against them (see `scenario_labels.md`), a recurring set of maneuvers
and conflicts showed up across all four datasets that none of the 44
types describe, forcing them into "No strong match" or a Weak-confidence
fallback. Types 45-50 were drafted to close those gaps, continuing the
existing numbering scheme. Type 51 (HeavyVehicle) was added afterward,
specifically to resolve the one remaining unmatched `image-only/`
scenario (`DetectAndRespondToSchoolBus`) — unlike 45-50, it's not drawn
from a recurring pattern across all five directories, just this single
case, so treat its generality with more caution than the others. None
of types 45-51 are part of the official Bench2Drive benchmark — they
are project-specific additions. Types 52-53 (JunctionEntryCutIn,
HazardAvoidanceCutIn) were added later still, splitting off two
recurring sub-patterns of StaticCutIn (4) that had grown to dominate the
type. Type 54 (EmergencyObstacleAvoidance) splits off a sub-pattern of
HardBrake (16): scenarios with no lead vehicle at all, or a non-vehicle
hazard, as opposed to a lead vehicle actively decelerating. Types 55-56
(OvertakingIntoOncomingLane, UnintentionalLaneDrift) split off two
sub-patterns of LaneChange (43): an overtake that explicitly crosses
into opposing traffic, and an unintentional drift, as opposed to a
deliberate same-direction lane-change maneuver. Types 57-58
(OncomingLeftTurnAcrossPath, PerpendicularCrossingConflict) split off
two recurring geometries of OppositeVehicleRunningRedLight (37) /
OppositeVehicleTakingPriority (38): an oncoming vehicle turning left
directly across the ego's straight path, and a vehicle crossing from a
perpendicular side street, as opposed to the dense multi-conflict or
adversary-u-turn scenes that still fall back to 37/38. See `plan.md` for
the full rationale on all of these.

| # | Scenario Name | Definition / Description |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| 45 | **ReversingManeuver** | The ego vehicle backs up (e.g., leaving a parking space, driveway, or alley) or must react to an adversary vehicle reversing toward or across its path, requiring low-speed maneuvering to avoid a collision with the reversing party. |
| 46 | **UTurn** | The ego vehicle executes a u-turn at a junction or mid-block, negotiating oncoming, crossing, or following traffic that conflicts with the reversal of direction. |
| 47 | **ParallelLaneTraffic** | The ego vehicle travels straight alongside an adversary vehicle moving in the same direction in a parallel lane, with no cut-in, crossing, or braking conflict between them — a benign co-travel baseline. |
| 48 | **AdversaryCutOut** | A leading or adjacent adversary vehicle voluntarily exits or diverges away from the ego's lane or path — the inverse of StaticCutIn/HighwayCutIn — requiring the ego to monitor but not necessarily brake or maneuver. |
| 49 | **RoundaboutNavigation** | The ego vehicle enters, circulates within, or exits a roundabout junction, yielding to or coordinating with adversary vehicles entering or already circulating in the roundabout. |
| 50 | **AnimalOnRoad** | An animal unexpectedly enters or is present on the roadway, requiring the ego vehicle to brake or maneuver to avoid a collision, at a junction or non-junction location. |
| 51 | **HeavyVehicle** | A basic scenario for the ego vehicle to learn to respond to heavy vehicles. |
| 52 | **JunctionEntryCutIn** | An adversary vehicle turns from a cross-street, ramp, or side road into the ego vehicle's lane at or near a junction, becoming a leading vehicle the ego must follow at a safe distance. |
| 53 | **HazardAvoidanceCutIn** | An adversary vehicle cuts into the ego vehicle's lane specifically to avoid an obstacle, hazard, or pedestrian in its own lane, requiring the ego to react to the sudden lane change. |
| 54 | **EmergencyObstacleAvoidance** | The ego vehicle must brake or swerve suddenly for a hazard, obstacle, or non-vehicle actor (e.g., a cyclist, or a motorcyclist that has lost control) directly in its path, with no other vehicle actively decelerating as the stated trigger. |
| 55 | **OvertakingIntoOncomingLane** | The ego vehicle passes a slower vehicle ahead by crossing into the opposing lane of traffic, distinct from a same-direction lane change. |
| 56 | **UnintentionalLaneDrift** | A vehicle drifts out of its lane and encroaches into an adjacent lane without an intentional lane-change maneuver. |
| 57 | **OncomingLeftTurnAcrossPath** | An oncoming vehicle turns left directly across the ego vehicle's straight-ahead path, at a signalized or non-signalized junction. |
| 58 | **PerpendicularCrossingConflict** | A vehicle crosses the ego vehicle's straight path from a perpendicular side street, running a red light or violating a stop/yield sign. |
